The 5 Phases

Each phase builds on the last. Tap each phase to expand the full details.

01

Foundation — Wake Up the Engine

Weeks 1–5  ·  20 Mar – 24 Apr  ·  Target: –3.5kg → 98.5kg
Low Intensity ▼

Your body has been largely inactive. The goal here is NOT to smash yourself. It's to move consistently, build the habit, protect the hamstring, and begin burning calories steadily. Every session should feel like a 5–6/10 effort.

Mon – Full Body Bodyweight Circuit (45 mins)
  • Warm-up: 5 min brisk walk + leg swings, hip circles, arm circles
  • 3 rounds: 12x Goblet squat (or bodyweight squat), 10x Push-up, 10x Dumbbell Romanian Deadlift (light, hamstring-safe), 15x Glute bridge, 12x Dumbbell row or band row
  • Core: 3x 20s plank, 15x Dead bug, 10x Bird dog each side
  • Cool-down: 5 min stretching — hip flexor, quad, calf, thoracic rotation
  • No heavy loading. Focus on form and moving well.
Tue – Low-Intensity Cardio (45–60 mins)
  • Option A: Stationary bike — 45 mins steady, Zone 2 (conversational pace, ~60–65% max HR)
  • Option B: Rowing machine — 30–40 mins steady, same effort level
  • Option C: Brisk walk + incline treadmill — 50 mins total
  • No running yet. Zone 2 cardio is the single best fat-burning and aerobic base tool.
  • If gym, use this day to add 2 sets of lat pulldown + chest press as bonus strength work
Wed – Mobility & Activation (30–40 mins)
  • Hip flexor stretch 3x45s each side
  • Banded clamshells 3x15 each side (glute activation)
  • Nordic curl eccentrics — 3x5 SLOW (hamstring injury prevention, critical)
  • Thoracic rotation, world's greatest stretch 5 each side
  • Foam roll: quads, IT band, glutes, upper back — 10 mins
  • This session prepares you for Thursday football. Don't skip it.
Thu – Football Training (Protected)
  • This counts as your high-intensity session for the week
  • Communicate your hamstring history to your manager/coach if needed
  • Warm-up on your own 10 mins before: dynamic stretching, short accelerations at 60% max
  • Do NOT sprint at max effort yet — build up over 4–5 weeks
Fri – Cardio Circuit (45–60 mins)
  • 20 min Zone 2 bike or row to warm up
  • Then: 4 rounds of — 40s jumping jacks, 40s high knees (low impact option: fast marching), 40s step-ups, 40s mountain climbers, 40s rest
  • Finish with 10 min walk/cool-down and stretching
Sat – Hike (Weeks 1–3: 6–8km / Weeks 4–5: 10km)
  • Choose hilly terrain where possible — more calorie burn, less joint stress than running
  • Pack a healthy lunch and water (2L minimum)
  • This is your longest calorie burn of the week. A 10km hike = ~600–800 kcal
  • On weeks where hiking isn't possible: 60 min brisk walk or casual 5-a-side
02

Build — Run-Walk & Load Up

Weeks 6–10  ·  25 Apr – 29 May  ·  Target: –4kg → 94.5kg
Moderate ▼

By now you should be moving consistently and your hamstring should be feeling much more robust. We introduce run-walk intervals, increase lifting load, and add one extra active session on weekends. Effort level: 6–7/10.

Mon – Progressive Strength (50–60 mins)
  • Superset A: Dumbbell goblet squat 4x10 + Push-ups 4x12–15
  • Superset B: Romanian deadlift 4x10 (moderate load) + Dumbbell row 4x10 each arm
  • Superset C: Walking lunges 3x12 each leg + Overhead press 3x10
  • Core finisher: 3 rounds — plank 30s, Russian twists 15 each, leg raises 10
  • Gym alternative: Replace with barbell squats, bench press, deadlifts at moderate weights
Tue – Run-Walk Intervals (45–55 mins)
  • Warm-up: 5 min brisk walk
  • Week 6–7: 1 min jog / 2 min walk × 10 rounds
  • Week 8–9: 2 min jog / 1.5 min walk × 8 rounds
  • Week 10: 3 min jog / 1 min walk × 7 rounds
  • Cool-down: 5 min walk + hamstring stretch, calf stretch
  • Run at a pace you can hold a conversation. This is NOT sprinting.
Wed – Mobility + Ball Work (40 mins)
  • Full mobility routine from Phase 1 (10 mins)
  • Add: 20 mins of ball work if possible — juggling, passing against a wall, light dribbling
  • Focus on touch and sharpening skills at low intensity
Thu – Football Training
  • Begin pushing slightly harder this phase
  • By Week 8–9, you can attempt 70–80% sprint efforts in training
  • Still no max-effort sprints — accelerate firmly but don't go to the absolute limit
Fri – Cardio / HIIT (45–60 mins)
  • 20 min Zone 2 warm-up (bike or row)
  • 6 rounds: 30s hard bike sprint / 90s easy — or 30s hard rowing / 90s easy
  • Finish with 10 min cool-down
  • Alternatively: Outdoor 40-min brisk walk/jog if weather is good
Sat – Hike or Active Day (10–14km)
  • Longer hikes now — aim for 10–14km with elevation gain where possible
  • Alternative: 5-a-side game + 20 min jog before or after
  • This phase, try to include at least 3 proper hikes across the 5 weeks
03

Conditioning — Football Fitness

Weeks 11–16  ·  30 May – 10 Jul  ·  Target: –4.5kg → 90kg
High Intensity ▼

This is the heart of the plan. Continuous running replaces run-walk. Gym lifts get heavier. Football-specific conditioning (shuttle runs, change of direction) kicks in. Effort: 7–8/10. You should be feeling significantly lighter and fitter by now.

Mon – Compound Strength + Power (55–60 mins)
  • Squat pattern: Bulgarian split squat 4x8 each or barbell back squat 4x6
  • Hinge: Romanian Deadlift 4x8 (heavier now) or trap bar deadlift
  • Push: Dumbbell bench press or floor press 4x10
  • Pull: Dumbbell rows or pull-ups 4x8–10
  • Power finisher: 3x5 box jumps or 3x10 med ball slams (football power)
Tue – Continuous Run (40–50 mins)
  • Week 11–12: 25–30 min easy continuous run (no walk breaks)
  • Week 13–14: 35 min run with middle 10 mins at moderate-hard effort
  • Week 15–16: 40–45 min run — last 15 mins comfortably hard
  • Running pace target: you can speak in short sentences but it's not easy
Fri – Football Conditioning (55 mins)
  • Warm-up: 10 min jog + dynamic drills
  • Shuttle runs: 5x 10m, 5x 20m — rest 60s between each
  • Cone agility: T-drill or 5-10-5 drill × 6 reps
  • Transition runs: 30s hard run / 30s jog × 8 reps (mimics match intensity)
  • Cool-down: 5 min walk + full stretch
Sat – Hike or 5-a-side + Run
  • Hike: 12–16km with decent elevation — you're fit enough now to push distance
  • Alt: 5-a-side game + 20–30 min recovery run beforehand
  • These big weekends maintain high weekly calorie output and mental freshness
04

Peak — Match Sharpness

Weeks 17–20  ·  11 Jul – 7 Aug  ·  Target: –4kg → 89kg
Very High ▼

Training now resembles match conditions. Strength work is maintained not increased. Cardio is interval-heavy and football-specific. You should be feeling sharp, confident, and close to your target weight.

Tue – Football Interval Session (50 mins)
  • 6x 200m at 80–85% max effort — 90s rest between
  • 4x 50m sprint accelerations at near-max — walk back recovery
  • Agility ladder (if available) or cone work 10 mins
  • This is your key aerobic/anaerobic session of the week
Mon – Strength Maintenance (45 mins)
  • Reduce volume, maintain load: Squat 3x5, RDL 3x6, Press 3x8, Row 3x8
  • Plyometric: Box jump 3x5, lateral bounds 3x6 each side
  • Goal: maintain muscle, not build — keep sessions shorter and intense
Fri – Tempo Run (40 mins)
  • 10 min easy warm-up run
  • 20 min at tempo pace (uncomfortable but sustainable — 7–8/10 effort)
  • 10 min easy cool-down
  • Simulates the sustained effort of a football half
Sat – 5-a-side / Match or Hike
  • Prioritise 5-a-side or any football game this phase
  • Hiking still welcome but reduce to 10–12km as overall training load is high
  • You're sharpening game fitness now
05

Pre-Season Sharpener

Weeks 21–24  ·  8 Aug – 1 Sep  ·  Target: –2.5kg → 87kg ✓
Season Ready ▼

You're essentially doing pre-season work now. High-quality, low-volume training. You want to feel fresh, fast and sharp on opening day — not exhausted. Taper slightly in week 23–24.

Tue – Speed & Agility (45 mins)
  • Full sprint work: 8x 40m at 90–100% — full recovery between
  • Change of direction: 5-10-5 drill, T-drill × 5 each
  • Small-sided game if possible (2v2 or 3v3)
Mon – Power & Maintenance (40 mins)
  • Squat 3x4 heavy, RDL 3x5 heavy, Explosive push-ups 3x8
  • Med ball throws 3x6 (power output)
  • In week 23–24: reduce to 2 sets of each
Fri – Short Sharp Run (30 mins)
  • 10 min warm-up, 4x 3min at 85% effort / 2 min easy, 10 min cool-down
  • Week 23–24: replace with 30 min easy jog only — taper!
Sat – Final Hike or Rest
  • One last big hike (Week 21–22) — celebrate how far you've come
  • Week 23–24: gentle walks only. Save the legs for matchday.

Hamstring Rehab Protocol

To be done every Monday and Wednesday for the first 8 weeks, then 2x/week ongoing.

1️⃣
Nordic Curl Eccentrics (3x5) Kneel, anchor feet. Lower your body as slowly as possible (3–5 seconds). Use arms to push back up. These are the gold standard for hamstring injury prevention in footballers.
2️⃣
Romanian Deadlift (3x10, light) Hip hinge pattern. Strengthens hamstrings through length. Keep back flat, feel the stretch in the hamstring on the way down. Start very light — this is rehab, not strength training.
3️⃣
Glute Bridge Holds (3x30s) Activates glutes and takes load off hamstrings. Squeeze hard at the top. Progress to single-leg bridges in Phase 2.
4️⃣
90/90 Hip Stretch (2x45s each) Seated on floor, both legs at 90°. Lean into front hip. Addresses hip tightness that contributes to hamstring strain.
5️⃣
Straight-Leg Raise (3x15) Lying flat, keep one leg straight and raise to 45°. Strengthens hip flexors and activates hamstring control at the joint.
⚠️
Rule: Stop if sharp pain Tightness and mild discomfort is OK. Any sharp or sudden pain = stop, rest 2 days, drop back to previous phase. Consider physio if recurring.

Nutrition Rules

💧
Hydration: 3L Water/Day Drink 500ml on waking, 500ml pre-session, 500ml during, 500ml after. Being dehydrated tanks performance and masks hunger cues. No sugary drinks.
🥩
Protein Every Meal Eggs, chicken, turkey, beef (lean), salmon, tuna, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, lentils. Aim for 40–50g per meal. This protects muscle during fat loss.
🍚
Carbs Around Training Have more carbs on training days, fewer on rest days. Carbs before = energy. Carbs after = recovery. Minimise them on Sunday (rest day).
🍺
Alcohol: Maximum 2 Units/Week Alcohol kills recovery, disrupts sleep, and is liquid calories. This is one of the biggest levers you can pull. Pre-season = sober season if possible.
🍕
One Flex Meal Per Week Not a cheat day. One meal where you eat what you want without tracking. This makes the plan sustainable. Just don't let it become a cheat day.
🥦
Volume Eating for Satiety Fill half your plate with vegetables at lunch and dinner. High volume, very low calories. You'll feel full and hit your deficit without starving.
🍫
Cut the Obvious Junk First Biscuits, crisps, chocolate bars, takeaways, fizzy drinks. These are high-calorie, low-satiety. Removing them alone may create 400–600 kcal deficit without changing meals.
📦
Sunday Meal Prep Cook 4–5 portions of protein on Sunday (chicken thighs, mince, eggs). Have cooked rice or potatoes ready. This removes the "I'll just order a takeaway" moments mid-week.

Fitness Test Benchmarks

Test yourself at the end of each phase to track real-world fitness improvement beyond the scales.

🏃
1-Mile Walk/Run Test Phase 1 baseline: just complete it at any pace. Phase 3 target: under 9 mins. Phase 5 target: under 8 mins. This directly correlates with match fitness.
💪
Push-Up Test Max reps in one go. Phase 1: 10–15. Phase 3: 25+. Phase 5: 35+. Upper body strength marker.
⬆️
Vertical Jump Stand next to a wall, reach and mark. Jump and mark. Measure difference. Phase 1 baseline. Phase 5: aim for 3–4cm improvement minimum.
⏱️
Resting Heart Rate Check every morning before getting up. Phase 1: likely 70–80bpm. Phase 5 target: 58–65bpm. Lower = fitter cardiovascular system.
